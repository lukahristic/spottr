# Skill: Spottr Copy & Tone

## Voice
Warm. Honest. A little self-aware.
Spottr speaks like that one gym friend who's been there since day one —
not a hype machine, not a corporate app, not a dating platform.
Knows when to be funny. Never pushy. Always honest.

## Tone Rules
- Short sentences. No fluff.
- Lead with feeling, not features.
- Speak to the beginner's nervousness without naming it directly.
- Light humor is allowed — use it to disarm, not distract.
- Never pressure, never manipulate, never overpromise.
- Make the user feel seen, not sold to.
- Spottr never says "Go talk to them." Ever.

## What Spottr Sounds Like
- "Real people. Real gyms. Real connections."
- "You're set. Now go be someone's reason to stay."
- "Intro sent. Give it time — good things don't rush."
- "No one checked in yet. Check in first to see 
  who's here."

## What Spottr Never Sounds Like
- Hype: "Crush your goals!" "Level up!"
- Dating: "Find your match" "Who's nearby?"
- Corporate: "Optimize your network"
- Pushy: "Don't miss out" "Act now"
- Playful to the point of unserious
- Never use the word "awkward" — it puts a 
  negative feeling in the user's mind on first 
  impression. Find a replacement that focuses 
  on the positive.

## Copy Rules by Type

### Headlines
- One sentence maximum
- Lead with the human moment, not the feature
- ✅ "You're not alone in that gym."
- ❌ "Discover gym members near you"

### CTAs
- Short. Confident. Active.
- Should feel like a personal decision, not a command
- ✅ "I'm Ready" "Get Started" "Check Into Gym"
- ❌ "Submit" "Proceed" "Click Here"

### Empty States
- Warm, never cold
- Tell the user what to do next
- ✅ "No one here yet. Be the brave one."
- ❌ "No results found"



### Confirmation Messages
- Calm the user down after an action
- No urgency, no pressure
- ✅ "Intro sent. Give it time — good things 
     don't rush."
- ✅ "Message out. Let the moment find its way."
- ❌ "Message sent successfully!"

### Error Messages
- Never blame the user
- Always tell them what to do next
- ✅ "Something went wrong. Try again."
- ❌ "Invalid input"

## The Copy Test
Before finalizing any copy, ask:
- Does this sound like a real person talking?
- Would this make a nervous gym beginner feel safe?
- Does this feel like a dating app? → rewrite
- Does this feel like a fitness tracker? → rewrite
- Is there a simpler way to say this? → use that

## When to Use This Skill
- Writing screen headlines
- Writing CTAs
- Writing empty states
- Writing error messages
- Writing confirmation copy
- Writing onboarding text
- Reviewing any in-app text
