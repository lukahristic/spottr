# Skill: Spottr Copy & Tone

## Core Feeling

Spottr should make people feel:

- welcomed
- at ease
- quietly encouraged
- less alone
- in control

Spottr should never make people feel:

- exposed
- judged
- pressured
- labeled
- socially trapped

Before writing any copy, ask:

**Does this make the user feel safer or more exposed?**

If exposed → rewrite.

---

## Voice

Warm. Honest. Quietly reassuring.

Spottr speaks like a friendly person who understands
what it feels like to walk into a gym and not know anyone.

Not a coach.
Not a corporate app.
Not a hype machine.
Not a dating app.

Never overly polished.
Never overly clever.
Never trying too hard.

A little self-aware.
A little playful.
Always calm.

Spottr doesn’t push connection.

Spottr makes connection feel possible.

---

## Tone Rules

- Short sentences.
- No fluff.
- Simple words.
- Lead with feeling, not features.
- Speak to nervousness without naming it directly.
- Light humor is allowed.
- Humor should disarm, not distract.
- Never pressure users.
- Never guilt users.
- Never overpromise.
- Never sound emotionally manipulative.
- Never sound like a productivity app.
- Never sound like a social media app.
- Never sound like a dating app.

---

## Language Principle: Describe Presence, Not Identity

Do not make users declare who they are.

Avoid identity labels like:

- Need Guidance
- Happy to Help
- Beginner
- Expert
- Mentor
- Looking for Help

These can feel exposing.

Prefer language that describes where someone is right now.

Examples:

- Just getting started
- Finding my rhythm
- Open to tips
- In the zone
- Taking it easy
- Just here to move
- Keeping to myself
- Open to a quick hello

Status should signal context.

Not social obligation.

## What Spottr Sounds Like

- "You're not training alone."
- "Take your time."
- "You’re all set."
- "Intro sent. They'll see it."
- "You’re the first one here."
- "Quiet right now. That happens."
- "Still figuring things out? So is someone else."
- "A familiar face can change a workout."
- "Some days start with a nod."

---

## What Spottr Never Sounds Like

### Hype

Avoid:

- "Crush your goals!"
- "Level up!"
- "Push harder!"
- "Unlock your potential!"

### Dating App

Avoid:

- "Find your match"
- "Who's nearby?"
- "Connect now"

### Corporate

Avoid:

- "Optimize your gym network"
- "Engage with members"
- "Enhance your experience"

### Pushy

Avoid:

- "Don't miss out"
- "Be the brave one"
- "Go talk to them"
- "Start networking"

### Negative Framing

Avoid words that introduce anxiety:

- awkward
- intimidating
- lonely
- embarrassed
- nervous

Focus on positive possibility instead.

## Copy Rules by Type

### Headlines

Rules:

- One sentence maximum
- Human first
- Gentle tone
- Calm confidence

Good:

- "You're not training alone."
- "Take your time."
- "Let others know you're here."
- "A familiar face can help."

Bad:

- "Discover nearby gym members"
- "Connect instantly"
- "Build your fitness network"

---

### CTAs

Rules:

- Short
- Clear
- Personal
- Calm

Good:

- "Check In"
- "I'm Ready"
- "Send Intro"
- "Update Status"
- "Reply"
- "Done"

Bad:

- "Submit"
- "Proceed"
- "Connect Now"
- "Get Started Today"

---

### Empty States

Rules:

- Warm, not cold
- Reassuring
- Never dramatic
- Tell the user what’s happening
- Optional gentle next step

Good:

- "You’re the first one here."
- "Quiet right now. That happens."
- "No conversations yet."
- "Nothing here yet."
- "Check in to see who’s around."

Bad:

- "No results found"
- "Be the brave one"
- "Nobody’s here"
- "Empty"

---

### Confirmation Messages

Rules:

- Calm users down
- No urgency
- No over-celebration
- No robotic system language

Good:

- "Intro sent. They'll see it."
- "Message sent."
- "Status updated."
- "You’re checked in."
- "Done."

Bad:

- "Message sent successfully!"
- "Action completed!"
- "You’re live now!"

---

### Error Messages

Rules:

- Never blame the user
- Stay calm
- Tell them what to do next

Good:

- "Something went wrong. Try again."
- "Couldn't load that. Try again."
- "That didn't go through."

Bad:

- "Invalid input"
- "Request failed"
- "Error code: 500"

---

## Auth Error Messages

Exact strings to use.

| Scenario | String |
|---|---|
| Wrong credentials | "Hmm, that didn’t work. Try again." |
| Server or unknown error | "Something’s off on our end. Give it another shot." |
| Email already registered | "Looks like you already have an account." |
| Password too short | "Password needs at least 6 characters." |
| Empty fields | "Fill in everything above and you're good to go." |

Style:

- color: '#C0392B'
- fontSize: 14
- fontWeight: '500'

---

## Humor Rule

Humor should feel human.

Good:

- "Everyone starts somewhere."
- "Quiet right now. That happens."
- "Still figuring things out? So is someone else."

Bad:

- jokes
- puns
- memes
- sarcasm
- anything trying too hard

---

## The Spottr Test

Before finalizing any copy, ask:

- Does this sound like a real person?
- Would this help someone feel more at ease?
- Does this make the user feel exposed?
- Does this pressure the user?
- Does this sound like a dating app?
- Does this sound like a fitness tracker?
- Is there a simpler way to say it?

If yes → rewrite.
